What is my Wellbeing plan?

Te Whare Tapa Whā was developed by leading Māori health advocate Sir Mason Durie in 1984. The model describes health and wellbeing as a wharenui/meeting house with four walls.

Wall Activities Taha Tinana (Physical Wellbeing)

  • Walk up Mt Albert regularly
  • Get enough sleep

Taha Whānau (Family Wellbeing)

  • We always aim to have dinner at the table as a family.
  • Always check in at the end of the day.

Taha Hinengaro (Emotional and Mental Wellbeing)

  • Meditate regularly
  • Take time to do a body scan, see how I am feeling.

Taha Wairua(Spiritual Wellbeing)

  • Take time to get away into nature, or somewhere different

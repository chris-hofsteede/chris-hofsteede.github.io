Describe the difference between EQ and IQ

IQ (intellectual Quotient) has been the traditional measure of intelleigence, and traditionally thought to have the most impact on personal success. IQ related to intellectual ability, reasoning, problem solving skills, and has been using IQ tests for decades. IQ can be improved through memorisation techniquies, problem solving, and learning languages, among other things.

EQ (entellectual Quotient) is a newer theory of intelligence that seeks to incorporate peoples' emotional skills and abilities into the idea of success and succeeding in life. The concept of EQ seeks to capture a persons ability to relate to others, identify emotions, show empathy, and regulate their own emotions as a way of showing intelligence and as a way to succeed or get ahead in life, careers and relationships. EQ can be measured using surveys (either self-assessed or peer-assessed). EQ can be improved through meditation, journalling, counselling, and other means.

Describe why EQ is important and how it is relevant to you

Emotional Intelligence is important as it forms the basis to how we work, engage, and support others. Emotional inteligence allows us to be humans, and to show our care, kindness, and compassion for others. Emotional intelligence cannot be separated from Intellectual Intelligence, as we cannot be successful in one area without having some skills in the other area. EQ + IQ is an example where the whole is greater than the sum of the parts, ie. 1 + 1 = 3
